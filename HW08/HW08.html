Homework 8: Road Kills in California

Valen Zhang

Everytime I drive on i40, I'll see some road kills. That makes me reflect on why there are more road kills at some locations than the other? This is why I decided to do explore road kills in California. California has many National Parks and State Parks. In this homeowkr, I decided to study the relationship between road kills and State Parks instead of National Parks because around National Parks, people are generally more prudent when they're driving. On the other hand, State Parks are everywhere and more spread out than the National Parks. Therefore, I believe that State Parks might be something interesting to study. I first imported the csv datafile for road kill data from FatalEncounters.org. Then, I imported the State Park boundaries from California Open Data webpage. In QGIS, I created a 10 mile buffer and a 3 mile buffer from each state park. Then, using the 3 mile and 10 mile buffer, I was able to select those road kills that are within those range using intersection function in QGIS. Results are shown below.
